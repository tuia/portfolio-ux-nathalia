The Problem

Planeta Y is a social network management tool for educational institutions, but since it wasn’t the company’s main product and the team didn’t have much knowledge in design, the software was very hard to use, to read and had an outdated interface.

The Solution

At first, I identified several easy-to-fix usability issues by conducting a heuristic review. Then, I made interviews with users from all the system roles to identify their needs and the system weaknesses, strengths and opportunities. With the discoveries made, I created personas to better focus on the system goals when prototyping and designing it.

One of the solutions to the system analysts (one of the personas created) was to make the system feel more personal by using bigger pictures and creating a page called "people", where they can check their prospects, leads and everyone who spoke about the education institution. Another solution made in this way was to de-bureaucratize some of the entities names, making it friendlier: “register user” became “add person”, for example, which feel more natural to these users.

For the managers, I presented two solutions: the first one was the reports screen, highly customisable, with the possibility of presenting, exporting and also saving the reports configurations. The second one was the news ticker, for marketing managers to see real-time quotes and data by distance on TV screens.

This project was successful, the final users who tested the new software were very excited and the sales increased.
