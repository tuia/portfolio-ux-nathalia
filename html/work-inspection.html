The Problem

Axxiom software company used to have the best softwares for electric energy distribution and related services on the 90s, but when they made a new and web version of it, the software didn’t go well because of usability matters. It was made by developers with almost no user-centered design (UCD) culture. They hired us to help them design more usable and easy to understand products. Inspection is one of the applications we designed, used on 7’’ or larger tablets by electricity technicians to check and repair the equipments on the streets.

The Solution

One of the solutions was to create customizable groups of services, so de technicians could request a variety of equipment and services easily, decreasing the time spent on the system. Another solution was modeling the sync configurations, making its status always visible on the lateral menu. This way, the user can work even in places that has poor or no connection at all, and synchronize automatically when they’re connected again. The different colours for the task status provide easy feedback by just looking at it. These colors are also used on the desktop application that manages the materials and services the technicians request. For the inspection task flow, I also focused on helping the user with information and feedback, using resources such as tips on the bottom of the windows and colour as information. When the user starts the inspection, the time he spends on it is counted, helping the managers identify problems even from the desktop application and increasing productivity.
