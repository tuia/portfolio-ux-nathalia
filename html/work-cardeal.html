The Problem

ADS Prev manages and develops social security systems of big companies such as USIMINAS. When they updated their main product, called Cardeal, moving from a desktop to web application, they wanted a modern interface rich in usability.

The Solution

The first step was to analyse the old desktop application screens, together with the company, to have a general view of the tool’s concept. We then visited their user's workplace for 2 days, observing the software usage and interviewing some of them. We identified the company's departments, the user's work routine according to each department, the problems and benefits they had when using the application, between other topics.

Based on the interviews, we established altogether with the client a new information architecture and also raised functionalities that would enter and leave the interface, accordingly to the user's needs. From there, I created prototypes and evaluated them with some users. Then I developed the front-end code of the main screens. I also delivered an interaction style manual, where the system components and best practices for developing the other screens are described.

The crucial point of improvement was the navigability of the system. Users complained of spending a lot of time snooping around and wondering where to go. The new information architecture turned from task-oriented to entity-oriented. In addition, we've added an always visible global search. The new forms also came to be more intuitive by demonstrating similar information grouped, highlighted entity status information (identified by specific colors) and a fixed lateral navigation.
