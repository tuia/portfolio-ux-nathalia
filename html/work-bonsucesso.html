The Problem

Bonsucesso bank used to invest a lot of money in their phone and mail clerk services. They decided to create a more functional homebank website, so the clients could solve their issues online. The platform also needed to be responsive and to showcase and sell other products.

The Solution

As a solution for the phone and mail services, we made an always visible customer service section on the interface, and also the "online invoice", with a green background and a leaf icon to represent the sustainability of the web service. We've hidden some information for security measures: the user's private information shouldn't be always visible, unless he wanted it to. Security was a major issue and the company had very strict rules for it.

Moving to prototypes and then UI design, It was time to conduct 5 usability tests with some of their users. There, I discovered that all the users found the interface aesthetically pleasing and even mentioned ease of use, but they also mentioned lack of feedback, especially when creating a new account. With the information I got from the tests, it was possible to make improvements on the final interface design, like making the sign up process more visible and providing the users the information they needed. This way, it was possible to reduce the number of support calls and printed paper invoices sent to the customer.
